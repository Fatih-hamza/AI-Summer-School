Learning Backward Compatible Representations

In today's multimedia-rich environment, the rapid growth of data poses significant challenges for developing efficient multi-modal retrieval systems. These systems are essential for retrieving relevant data like text, images, audio, and video. As data continues to expand, scalable and high-performance retrieval systems are increasingly necessary, driving advancements in technology. Embedding-based deep neural networks (DNNs) have become a key solution, transforming high-dimensional data into lower-dimensional embeddings that capture semantic meanings and enable easy comparison and retrieval. However, a major challenge is that when a DNN is updated, the internal representation changes, which complicates updating retrieval systems with new models. This requires re-indexing, namely to re-extract new feature representations for all the data in the gallery-set. Backward-compatible representation learning addresses this challenge. It enables new models to generate embeddings that remain comparable with those from earlier models, allowing updated systems to interact with existing gallery data without full reprocessing. We will discuss the subject of compatibility with reference to the recent literature and new findings.

Design Thinking for Human-Centered AI Development

As artificial intelligence increasingly influences decision-making, services, and user experiences, the need to design AI systems that are aligned with real human needs has become critical. This hands-on workshop introduces participants to Design Thinking as a practical, human-centered framework for guiding the development of AI solutions that are ethical, inclusive, and user-driven. Through a combination of short lectures, interactive exercises, and group collaboration, participants will explore how to apply the five stages of Design Thinking: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test, to real-world AI challenges. Emphasis will be placed on integrating diverse perspectives, surfacing hidden biases, and ensuring that AI systems reflect the values and expectations of the communities they serve. By the end of the workshop, participants will have gained practical experience with design thinking tools tailored to AI project workflows, learned how to frame AI development around user needs and ethical considerations, and developed a rapid prototype addressing a human-centered AI challenge. This workshop is ideal for AI practitioners, designers, developers, researchers, and policy professionals seeking to bridge the gap between technical development and responsible innovation.

Generating Synthetic Face and Body Models

Generating content in 3D is of particular importance for the promise to reduce the cost and development time in many domains like for movie industries, Virtual/Augmented reality, videogames, and humans/avatars interaction. In this session, some recent results in generating synthetic face and body avatars in 3D will be presented. First, the task of 3D talking heads generation will be introduced, by discussing the latest advancements that also work with emotions, unconstrained topologies, and multi-languages. Then, the task of synthetizing complex behaviors of 3D bodies from text prompts will be addressed, with a specific emphasis on training free solutions, and approaches that also contaminate diffusion models with reinforcement learning in the loop.

Efficient Speech Generative Modeling With Little Tokenization

The field of speech generation typically assumes speech must be tokenized for transformer-based generative models, often using complex semantic and acoustic tokens. This talk challenges this assumption by introducing a simpler approach called discretized Mel-spectrograms (dMel), which quantizes standard Mel-spectrograms into discrete values. This technique, when used with transformer models, allows for the creation of effective text-to-speech systems without complex tokenization. The approach is extended to multi-modal generation, enabling models to produce speech from both text and video inputs. A key highlight is that dMel facilitates efficient streaming text-to-speech generation with minimal latency, opening doors for real-time applications.
